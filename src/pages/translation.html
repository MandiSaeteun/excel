Frequently Asked Questions

  • 1. Is the interpreter certified?

    Interpreter certification can be critical and it can influence outcomes. If your case requires the use of a Court Certified or a Court Registered Interpreter, and you know what you are doing, you can search for someone and hire directly; on your own, from the appropriate state interpreter registry. If you choose to hire through a language service provider (LSP), make sure you ask for the interpreter’s name and cross reference the interpreter with corresponding California interpreter registries to ensure they are in fact licensed by the state (and not “certified” by a language company). The quality is not the same. Avoid compromising the integrity of your case by knowing the difference.

  • 2. What is the cost of interpretation services?

    Factors that influence interpreter fees include language, availability of certified and licensed interpreters in specific industries within specific regions of the state, duration of the job, setting, nature of the interpretation, mileage, travel time and/or lodging. Depending on the level of certification, there is usually a two hour to a half-day, industry practice, minimum requirement. Scheduling interpreter services in advance will help keep costs to a minimum.

  • 3. What industries do you cover?

    Our document translators are capable of general as well as industry-specific translations. Our extensive network of technical translators encompass specialty fields including legal, healthcare, education, social services, business and website localization.
